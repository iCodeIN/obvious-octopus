Detailed Description

HSV is one of the most common cylindrical-coordinate representations of points in an RGB color model. The two representations rearrange the geometry of RGB in an attempt to be more intuitive and perceptually relevant than the cartesian (cube) representation. Developed in the 1970s for computer graphics applications, HSV is used today in color pickers, in image editing software, and less commonly in image analysis and computer vision. HSV stands for hue, saturation, and value, and is also often called HSB (hue, saturation, brightness)
